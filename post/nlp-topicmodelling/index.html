Natural Language Processing with R: Topic Modelling - 622 U.S Presidential Speeches (1789-2010)

April 23, 2021

THIS IS A WORK IN PROGRESS

Source Code (Beta): https://rpubs.com/w85b/757696

United states of America (USA) plays an influential role at an international level in driving political and economic policies which change several nations. Political analysts and stock markets across the globe often keep a close watch on the USA strategies and their priorities to get early sign of upcoming changes and its impact on businesses and other countries. Speeches delivered by USA presidents has been one the quite common way to get early sense of government policies and priorities. But extraction of any hidden theme or messages from the speeches is very much dependent on the ability of an analyst and can often lead to wrong interpretation and be time consuming.

Natural Language processing (NLP) is a branch of machine learning technique which helps in extracting meaningful information from the human readable text. With the advancement in NLP, algorithms are being improvised regularly to extract hidden topics, theme from the single document or large set of documents. Topic modelling is one such technique used in NLP for the automatic analysis of text data to extract cluster of words which are closely related. This is an ‘unsupervised’ machine learning technique because it does not require a pre-defined set of topics that has been previously manually classified. This method helps to analyze vast number of documents in much faster way compare to manual analysis and helps in extraction of topics or themes which otherwise would not have been quite easy to find. NLP and in particular Topic modelling is already widely used in real life applications for the analysis of customer reviews, sentiment analysis, applications in chatbots, document categorization etc. This paper aims to contribute to automatic analysis of political speeches with the help of machine learning methods. The aim of the analysis is to extract the topics which are priority and focused areas for different USA presidents and find any associated similarity between them. Along with the topic modelling, the paper will also contribute to analyze the trends of topics over the period since the first USA president. We will use 2 main topic modelling techniques namely Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) and Structural Topic Model (STM). The dataset used in this paper is sourced from Miller Center which is an affiliate of University of Virginia and specializes in analysis of government policies and political history.
